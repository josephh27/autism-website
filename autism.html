Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurological and developmental disorder that affects how people interact with others, communicate, learn, and behave.
Types and levels of ASD There are numerous subtypes of autism, the majority of which are affected by a combination of genetic and environmental factors. DSM-4 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Disorders) divided autism into five types, ranging from Asperger's (now called Level 1 Autism, the mildest), to Childhood Disintegrative Disorder (most severe). Causes and risk factors of ASD The causes of ASD are complex and not completely understood, but researchers have identified a combination of genetic and environmental factors that may contribute to the development of the disorder. While the exact cause of ASD is still unknown, studies have identified a number of potential risk factors that may increase the likelihood of developing the disorder.
Diagnosis, signs, and symptoms
It is difficult to diagnose ASD because there is no medical test for its diagnosis. The child's developmental history and behavior are assessed to make a diagnosis. While ASD can be detected early on at the age of 18 months or younger, it may take years later for many children to receive a final diagnosis, meaning that they might not be able to get the early help they need.
